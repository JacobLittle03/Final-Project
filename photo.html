For the Saladman project, I took the starting photos, formed them into a humanoid shape, played with the hue and saturation, and ended up with Mr. Saladman, a corporate boss of a company that sells salad kits!

Color/Saturation Alteration (and Advertisement)

I took an image of a 1956 Chevrolet BelAir Convertible, selected all around the car with the magic wand and the lasso tool, copied and pasted it into a new layer, and the background was turned black and white. After that, for the advertisement, I put the 1940-1950's logo at the top left of the image.

Retouched Image

I used the clone stamp to repair spots with scratches and tears. Then I used the crop tool to cut off the top, as that whole section was unrecoverable.

Layer Mask

I created a layer mask featuring the creator of Steven Universe, Rebecca Sugar, by using the layer mask tool, then, I took the layer mask and pasted it over a background from Steven Universe Future.

Layer Blending Mode

With both images, I overlayed a texture with blending modes and used the eraser on the magma texture to make it only go over the doberman in image 2.

Adjustment Layer

I overlayed a laser texture and played around with hue and saturation on top of an image of a concert to amplify the environment.

Custom Text with Image

I used a text font, overlayed an image on top of it and added a stroke to add more dimension to the text.

Fanmade Poster

For this project, I used texture images to give the animatronics more texture, a layer mask for the human in the image, layer blending mode for a majority of assets in this image, adjustment layers to alter some of the assets' hues, a clipping mask, text with an overlayed texture, and a variety of layer styles to amplify the text.

Collage Images

For this project, I used multiple images from various Chuck E. Cheese's locations and the Freddy Fazbear's Pizza Place sign from the Five Nights at Freddy's movie (2023), including extras like lightning strikes, a cop car, and so on. I used a variety of blending modes, adjusting hues & saturations, and so on to make this location look as real as possible.

Here, I took the final product and applied a couple filters to make the image appear as old, plus making the image grayscale without drowning out any of the actual details.
